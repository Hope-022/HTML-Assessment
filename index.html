Egusi Soup Recipe

Nigerian egusi soup can also be called "Ofe egusi" is a delicious and versatile soup which goes well with eba, fufu and pounded yam


Prep Time

20 mins

Cook Time

1hr 10mins

Total Time

1hr 30mins

Servings

8

Difficulty

Medium


Ingredients

  • 1 Red bell pepper
  • 2 Scotch bonnet pepper
  • 2 Onion
  • ¼ cup Palm oil
  • 1 tablespoon Fermented locust beans
  • 3 tablespoons Ground crayfish
  • 1 cup Dried Fish
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 ½ cup Egusi
  • 3 fillets Smoked mackerel
  • 1 cup Mixed seafood
  • 1 bunch Kale

Instructions

  1. Blend red bell pepper, scotch bonnet pepper and one onion in a food processor with a cup of water.

  2. Defrost the frozen seafood. Wash, strip and slice the kale; chop the remaining onion. Cut the smoked mackerel fillets into smaller pieces. Keep to one side.

  3. Heat the oil in a saucepan for 30 secs, add the chopped onions. Fry until the onions appear translucent, then add the locust beans. Stir for one minute to prevent sticking.

  4. Add the blended pepper mix and leave to cook for around 15 mins, stirring occasionally, then add some water into the pot (3-4 cups). Pour in the dried fish and ground crayfish with some a little salt to taste. Smoked mackerel is salty, and will make the soup more salty, so make allowance for that. Leave to simmer for 5 mins.

  5. Take 2 tablespoons of the cooking sauce and add into the egusi. Mix well to make a thick paste. Use your hands to make small egusi balls before dropping into the pan.

  6. Reduce the heat and allow to cook for at least 20 mins.

  7. Mix in kale and smoked mackerel. If you think the soup is too thick, add some more water. Leave to simmer for 10 mins, still under low heat.

  8. Add the seafood and leave for 5 mins.

  9. Remove from heat and serve.

Important Tip:Use fresh vegetables for better taste.
